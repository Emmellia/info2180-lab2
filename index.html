The Beauty of Music

October 1, 2020 by Emmellia Hayden

Music is one of the freest form of self expression. Music is a universal language understood by many. Music takes you out our head and create a space for you to be whoever you want to be. Music is emboding and often cannot be put into words. It is captivating and is a medium for healing. Music is often used for therapy to help persons overcome traumatic events. Music therapy is the clinical and evidence-based use of music interventions to accomplish individualized goals within a therapeutic relationship. Music therapy incorporates techniques such as listening to, reflecting on, and creating music to improve a clientâ€™s health and well-being. Immersing people in music can allow them to more easily express themselves, identify and process difficult experiences, develop social and communication skills, or simply find emotional release.

Music therapy can be used for a variety of conditions, and with adults or children. The practice helps people suffering from anxiety, depression, and trauma to illuminate or express underlying sources of pain. Those with autism can improve the ability to communicate and socialize through music therapy's structured setting, activities, and relationship. The diverse nature of music means it can be applied in the treatment of concerns both physical and psychological.
